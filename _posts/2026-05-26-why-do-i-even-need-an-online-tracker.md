**Question:** Why do I need an online tracker?

**Short Answer:** You don't

**Longer Answer:** You might

This article provides a few example use cases for an Online Tracker in Second Life.

## Use Case: Trying to Make Friends

Second Life residents are notified when their friends login or logoff, if the friend has that enabled.

No online status notification is provided for residents who are not in your friends list
and friends who have not enabled online status notification

Contacting and meeting other residents who are not in your friends list can be difficult.
You can IM them or send a notecard but they may be offline and if/when they do respond,
you may be offline. An interactive real-time engagement requires both parties to be online.

> Knowing when someone is online makes it easier to engage with them
{: .prompt-info }

Maybe you would prefer to send a notecard rather than IM if someone is offline.

> Knowing if someone is online or offline can help you decide how to engage with them
{: .prompt-info }

## Use Case: Service Relationship

Perhaps you are in a role that requires some form of service, an action or message that needs
to be performed when another resident comes online. Even if you are friends with that resident
you may not see the notification of their login in your Local Chat window or you may be offline.

An online tracker like the `Discord IM Online Tracker` can send an Instant Message to alert you
if you are online and/or a Discord post to alert you if you are offline. An IM is much more
likely to get noticed and the Discord online status messages can serve to alert you when offline.
If you have IM to Email notification configured then the IM may also serve to alert when offline.

> Instant Messages and Discord Posts provide a more efficient alerting mechanism
{: .prompt-info }

## Use Case: Accept Response Required

If you need another resident to respond with an acceptance dialog or permission dialog then they
cannot do that when offline. For example, to invite someone to a group.

> Knowing when a resident is online can assist with when to make requests that require acceptance
{: .prompt-info }

## Use Case: Scheduling Roles

Perhaps you manage events or a club or a role playing activity and need to be able to schedule
when your employees or co-conspirators will be available to participate or serve in some capacity.

An online tracker that maintains a history of online status for those residents can provide you with
some awareness of their online presence patterns.

> Knowledge of a resident's online presence patterns can assist with scheduling activities
{: .prompt-info }

## Use Case: Monitoring Bots

Scripted Agents (Bots) in Second Life often need to be online continually. They may be serving as a host
or greeter or store assistant and should always be online. However, servers sometimes go down, the Bot's
service running on another computer somewhere may stop unexpectedly, stuff happens.

An online tracker for your Bots can provide immediate notification of a drop in service and the need to
perform some remedial action like restarting the Bot's server or service.

> An online tracker can assist with maintaining continual online presence of a scripted agent
{: .prompt-info }

## Use Case: Combat

In some forms of combat in Second Life groups engage with each other to compete over some goal.
Often that engagement is face-to-face in real-time. It may be helpful to know when your enemy
is online in order to engage them in battle or avoid battle. It may be helpful to know when your
enemy is offline in order to perform some reconnaissance, subterfuge, surveillance, or activity
unhindered by enemy presence.

> Knowledge of an opponent's online status may provide some advantage in gaming scenarios
{: .prompt-info }
